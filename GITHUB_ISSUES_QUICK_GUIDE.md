## ‚ö° 30-Minute Quick Win

### Step 1: Sample 10-15 Representative Issues (15 min)

Visit: https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues?q=state%3Aopen%20label%3Adocumentation

**What to look for:**
- 3-4 "missing documentation" issues
- 2-3 "outdated/incorrect" issues  
- 2-3 "unclear/confusing" issues
- 1-2 "can't find info" issues
- 1-2 "missing context" issues

**How to choose:**
- Issues with most üëç reactions (shows user impact)
- Issues with multiple comments (shows active pain)
- Mix of old and new issues (shows pattern over time)
- Issues you could actually solve (be realistic)

**Save:** Issue number, title, category, upvotes

---

### Step 2: Categorize & Prioritize (10 min)

Create simple spreadsheet:

| Issue # | Title | Category | Upvotes | Your Priority |
|---------|-------|----------|---------|---------------|
| #1234 | No docs for X | Missing | 15 | CRITICAL |
| #5678 | Example broken | Incorrect | 8 | CRITICAL |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Priority criteria:**
- CRITICAL: Actively hurting users (broken examples, missing docs for released features)
- HIGH: Slowing users down (unclear explanations, hard to find)
- MEDIUM: Nice to have (missing edge cases, could be clearer)

---

### Step 3: Quick Analysis (5 min)

Answer these questions:
1. **Pattern**: Do issues cluster? (Most are "missing docs"? Most are "outdated"?)
2. **Root cause**: Why did these issues happen? (No docs in release process? No review cycle?)
3. **Systematic fix**: What prevents future issues? (Process change? Templates? Automation?)

Write 2-3 sentences on each.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è WHAT NOT TO DO

‚ùå **Don't:** Try to solve all 40+ issues
- **Why:** Overwhelming, impossible in reasonable time
- **Instead:** Sample 10-15, analyze patterns, propose systematic fixes

‚ùå **Don't:** Create detailed solutions for every issue  
- **Why:** Time-consuming, diminishing returns
- **Instead:** High-level categorization, priority framework, 1-2 deep examples

‚ùå **Don't:** Criticize Anthropic for having open issues
- **Why:** Every product has doc issues; you're applying to help fix them
- **Instead:** Frame as "opportunity to provide immediate value"

‚ùå **Don't:** Promise to fix everything in your application
- **Why:** Unrealistic, sets wrong expectations
- **Instead:** "I've prioritized the critical issues addressable in Week 1"

## ‚úÖ WHAT TO DO

‚úÖ **Sample selectively:** 10-15 representative issues showing patterns
‚úÖ **Analyze systematically:** Categorize, prioritize, identify root causes
‚úÖ **Propose strategically:** Quick wins + systematic fixes preventing recurrence
‚úÖ **Be specific:** Reference actual issue numbers for credibility
‚úÖ **Show process thinking:** Not just "fix issues" but "establish processes"
